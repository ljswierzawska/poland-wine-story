By Luiza Swierzawska

Climate change has changed the demographics of wine production. Rising temperatures have redefined the lines of winemaking regions, revealing some new contenders. In Europe, the shift was northwards with Poland leading this transformation.

As Victoria Brzezinski wrote for the British Vogue Magazine, it’s an unexpected turn of events seemingly far from “a land of potato and grain, tinnies of Tyskie and the clinks of vodka-filled shot glasses at every wedding.” But here we are, with over 700 registered wineries in Poland.

While this viticultural awakening may raise some eyebrows, Poland is not entirely new to winemaking. The country’s wine production history dates back to the Middle Ages, when it was quite developed, until the Little Ice Age – a period of cooling in the Northern Hemisphere. ​According to Wojciech Bońkowski, wine production in Poland was hampered once again in the 1960s. This time by the communist government, which prioritized the production of fruit wine and shut down major wineries.
